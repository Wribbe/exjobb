Title Evaluating web based interface design patterns for data visualization.
Author Stefan Eng - atn08sen@student.lu.se
Duration YYYY-MM ‚ü∂ YYYY-MM+3
Question How large of an impact do specific design-characteristics based on interface-design and cognition theory have on a users ability, when presented with data, to arrive at a specific conclusion?
Context The Cinematic producer wants a at-a-glance readable overview of how much of each team members total work-time has been assigned to tasks. I think this is a good opportunity to apply, test and evaluate different web-relevant user-interface design patterns in a filtered data-visualization setting.

Desired Effects

Academically Identifying popular user-interface designs from both academia and current trends. Design and perform tests in order to gather real-world user data to verify if the designs have the desired impact on the user experience.
Application functionality Present approximate overview of assigned-to-available time ratio for the majority of the team-members in a verifiable digestible manner to Cinematics producer.

Work-flows

Academic
  • Identify an applicable user interface pattern.
  • Apply the pattern to the design of the application.
  • Measure impact of the pattern.
  • Repeat with new pattern.
Application
  • Pick a user.
  • Check available hours based on contract and vacation.
  • Figure out how many of those hours are assigned in Shotgun and Jira.
  • Display relevant information in the application.

Resources

Data There are three systems that need to be accessed in order for the planned application to have the desired effect:
HRTB
Internal MASSIVE HR-tool that has data about contract-hours and vacation.
Shotgun
Content management and task-planning software used to assign and track tasks.
Jira
Issue tracking software developed by Atlassian.
Platform & Design Current design revolves around a light-weight webb-application created and hosted with Python and Flask.
